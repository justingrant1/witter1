The Beginning

Founded by a Legend

In 1959, legendary numismatist Dean Witter created an innovative coin album to hold US Proof Sets. With the success of the albums, the following year Dean decided to open Witter Coin in a small store on Bush and Kearny streets in San Francisco's Financial District.

Dean later moved to the historic Hobart Building on Market Street in 1982, cementing Witter Coin's place as a San Francisco institution.

Today, Witter Coin operates from its showroom at 2299 Lombard Street in the Marina/Cow Hollow District — a safe, welcoming space open to the public six days a week.

Meet Seth Chandler

A Lifelong Numismatist

Seth Chandler is the owner and chief numismatist of Witter Coin, America's coin shop. They have proudly served collectors since 1960 in San Francisco. Seth leads one of the most dynamic and community-focused coin shops in the country along with his 28 employees.

Seth is a lifelong numismatist who has been recognized multiple times by Coin World as one of the Top 100 Numismatists of All Time. He was also recognized as one of the "Greatest Coin Graders Of All Time" by COINage magazine. He has built some of the finest coin collections in the nation and is a contributor to the iconic Red Book, the world's best-selling price guide and reference for U.S. coins.

Seth is a co-founder and guest grader at CAC Grading, the world's finest coin grading and authentication company. He also serves on the Board of Governors for the American Numismatic Association (ANA) — the world's largest coin club. He's also an instructor for Advanced Coin Dealing at the ANA's prestigious Summer Seminar.

Seth also founded the San Francisco Coin Club, also known as "The Club with the Most Coolest Coin Nerds in the World." He is one of the top sellers of coins and bullion on the livestreaming platforms, Whatnot and eBay Live. He also created the most popular social media account for a coin shop, bringing numismatics to a whole new audience.

Seth is the creator of the annual San Francisco Coin Scavenger Hunt. Seth hides $50,000 worth of coins across iconic locations all over the city during National Coin Week. This draws coverage from all four local news stations as well as the international press.

"Whether it's a $1 coin or a $1,000,000 rarity — every collector deserves expert guidance and a fair deal."

— Seth Chandler, Owner

Giving Back

Witter Coin University

In 2021, Seth founded Witter Coin University (WCU), a nonprofit that brings Young Numismatists (ages 14–20) to San Francisco for an all-expense-paid week of hands-on learning, mentorship, and immersion in the world of coins.

Every year they raise over $200,000 to provide 40 scholarships to Young Numismatists. Seth's dedication to education extends to his shop, where he hires around 10 interns each summer to learn and work alongside the Witter Coin team.
